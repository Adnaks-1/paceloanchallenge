## Core Concept Summary
C-PACE (Commercial Property Assessed Clean Energy) financing in Minnesota is a
state-enabled mechanism allowing commercial property owners to finance eligible
energy, water, and resiliency improvements through a voluntary special assessment
attached to the property and repaid over a long tenor.

Repayment is tied to the property, not the owner, and is typically collected via
assessment billing mechanisms rather than traditional loan amortization.

---

## Legal Foundation (Minnesota-Specific)

### Enabling Structure
- Authorized under Minnesota statute via local implementing entities
- Operates through a voluntary special assessment framework
- Assessment is recorded against the property following owner petition and approval

### Key Legal Characteristics
- Obligation generally runs with the property
- Recorded assessment lien exists
- Assessment terms defined by program administrator and local authority
- Typically transferable upon sale (subject to lender and buyer consent)

### Mortgage Lender Consent
- Existing mortgage lender consent is almost always required
- Consent process addresses:
  - Lien priority concerns
  - Cure rights
  - Foreclosure mechanics
  - Transfer and payoff conditions

### Commercial vs Residential Distinction
- This skill applies ONLY to commercial / multifamily (C-PACE)
- Residential PACE has separate consumer protection laws and higher regulatory scrutiny
- Do not mix residential compliance logic into commercial workflows

## Eligible Use Cases (High-Level)
- Energy efficiency upgrades (HVAC, envelope, lighting)
- Renewable energy (solar, storage where permitted)
- Water efficiency improvements
- Building resiliency upgrades (program-dependent)

## Capital Stack Integration Patterns

### Pattern 1: Gap Capital / Equity Preservation
**Objective**
- Reduce sponsor equity contribution
- Finance long-life improvements off traditional loan balance sheet

**Stack Example**
- Senior mortgage
- C-PACE (subordinate but assessment-backed)
- Sponsor equity

**Key Advantage**
- Long tenor aligned with asset life
- Cash flow impact partially offset by energy savings

---

### Pattern 2: Incentive + Credit Stacking
**Objective**
- Improve project IRR and payback

**Stack Example**
- Senior debt
- C-PACE
- Utility rebates / incentives
- Federal tax credits (monetized separately)

**Best Practice**
- Treat incentives as principal reduction or post-install reimbursement
- Avoid double-counting savings in underwriting

---

### Pattern 3: Refinance / Recapitalization Strategy
**Objective**
- Fund upgrades without full refinance

**Stack Example**
- Existing senior loan
- Add-on C-PACE for capex

**Risk**
- Highest lender friction
- Requires strong savings model and consent narrative

## Legal & Deal Diligence Checklist

### Mandatory Checks
- Confirm local program participation (city/county adoption)
- Review implementing entity guidelines
- Verify assessment lien recording process
- Analyze title implications
- Obtain written mortgage lender consent

### Transfer & Exit Review
- Sale transferability language
- Refinance payoff requirements
- Acceleration or prepayment penalties
- Buyer and lender disclosure obligations

---

## Risk Considerations
- Lender refusal can kill deal late-stage
- Assessment lien visibility may affect buyer pool
- Misclassification of eligible improvements can invalidate financing
- Overstated energy savings undermine underwriting credibility

---

## When NOT to Use C-PACE
- Short hold period with no buyer appetite for assessments
- Hostile or inflexible senior lender
- Small projects where transaction cost outweighs benefit
- Assets with complex ownership or title issues
